Characters

the main characters and their roles in the book

heroes ⋇ enemies ⋇ everyone else

the heroes

Luke Skywalker
The hero. He is a Jedi Knight, aiding the Rebel Alliance whenever he must. He is Anakin's (Darth Vader's) son, and Leia's brother. They are twins. For more information, click here.
Has not died.
Princess Leia
Luke's sister, and Alliance leader. She lost her planet to the Empire, and is now a leader in the Alliance. For more information, click here.
Has not died.
Han Solo
Smuggler turned Alliance pilot. He is the pilot of the Millennium Falcon, and boasts that his ship has made the Kessel Run in under 12 parsecs. For more information, click here.
Has not died.
Chewbacca
Companion of Han Solo. He's a Wookie, and accompanies Han Solo as a fellow smuggler. Although he understands Basic, he speaks in Shyriiwook, making it difficult for others to understand him. For more information, click here.
Dies in the Yuuzhan Vong war.
C-3PO
The golden robot. He is a rule-follower, and loves to exaggerate things. He also seems to be proud of the fact he can speak over 6 billion languages, boasting this to anyone who will listen. For more information, click here.
Has not been disassembled permanently.
R2-D2
The trouble-making robot. Unlike his companion, he is extremely adventurous, breaking rules and what not. However, R2-D2 is a loyal friend to the others, even if he seems not to be. For more information, click here.
Has not been disassembled permanently.

the enemies

Emperor Palpatine
Evil's leader. He leads the Empire in their mission to rule the galaxy. He has been the master of many Sith apprentices, including Mara Jade, Darth Vader, Vergere, Darth Maul, and many others. Palpatine has had a life strongly influenced by the Sith, which explains the lack of good inside him. For more information, click here.
Original body is destroyed in the second Death Star.
Darth Vader
Father of good, apprentice of evil. Though he is Luke's father, he is Palpatine's apprentice, and commits several evil deeds throughout his service to him, killing many people. In the end, however, he kills Palpatine and returns to the light (good) side of the Force. For more information, click here.
Dies on the second Death Star
Jabba the Hutt
A gangster. He is insistent on capturing Han Solo in previous books, hangs him as a decoration, as Han became frozen in carbonite. Jabba tries to kill several of the heroes in the Rancor Pit, ending in the rancor's death. He then attempts to kill them through using the Sarlacc, but fails. For more information, click here.
Dies from being strangled by Leia.

other important characters

Boba Fett
A bounty hunter. He captures Han Solo on Cloud City, resulting in Jabba's wall decoration. He is from Kamino, and is Jango Fett's only unaltered clone. Although he was eaten by the Sarlacc, he was able to survive the Sarlacc's acids. For more information, click here.
Has not died.
Lando Calrissian
Han Solo's comrade. He is a bit of a gambler, resulting in the Millennium Falcon's changing owners. He plays a role in helping to free the heroes from Jabba's Palace, acting as a guard. For more information, click here.
Has not died.
The Ewoks (species)
A tiny race. These creatures are very small, and reside in the forests of Endor. Although they are small, they aid the Rebels in their path to victory. For more information, click here.
Die at various times.

Summary

a summary of the book

Han Solo, frozen in carbonite, is held as a wall decoration in Jabba's Palace. C-3PO and R2-D2 then travel across Tatooine to rescue him, but soon discover that they are to be Jabba's servants. Leia then poses as a bounty hunter to come rescue Han, but becomes a slave to Jabba. Luke finally comes to barter for them, but is then thrown into the Rancor Pit, though he kills the rancor. This group of heroes, including the droids, are taken on Jabba's Barge, where they either serve him, or are to be executed. Here, there appears a small battle between the two sides, resulting in the death of all aboard the barge, including Jabba. Leia strangles Jabba to make her escape, and aids the final destruction of the barge. The group of heroes escape from this conflict, at last freed.

Luke then travels to Dagobah to complete his training with Yoda, only to find that Yoda is dying. He soon dies of old age. Between these two events, Vader and the Emperor meet to discuss Luke, and what to do with him. Emperor Palpatine seems to decide that he will dispose of Vader upon Luke's arrival, as Luke would be the stronger of the two. Vader seems to have an internal conflict between the two -Emperor Palpatine, his master and Luke Skywalker, his son - acting unsure of who he will side with if he will have to choose. The Rebels then plan their attack on the second Death Star, resulting in the mission to Endor.

The Rebels pilot the Tydirium, and are granted passage to Endor. Upon landing, Han, Chewbacca, Luke, Leia, and the droids are attacked by Imperial troopers. Boarding speeder bikes, they attempt to escape the troopers. One of them shoots toward Leia, knocking her off the bike. The others continue without notice of this incident, losing her. When Leia falls, she is knocked out, and wakes up to encounter Wicket, an Ewok. They befriend each other. The others encounter the Ewoks in a very different way, through being caught. Han, Luke, Chewbacca, and R2-D2 are caught by the Ewoks, who find them to be the enemy. They are convinced that C-3PO is a deity of some sort. They are taken to the Ewok village, and meet Leia once again. After C-3PO persuades the Ewoks to free the others, they (the Rebels and C-3PO) tell them of the Empire and the Rebel's travels. On the Death Star, during these encounters, the Emperor and Vader meet. Vader is sent to Endor to wait for Luke to come to him, as the Emperor says that he will. Leia and Luke briefly talk before Luke goes to meet Vader. Luke tells her that they are siblings, and how Vader is their father. Luke then tells her of his mission to meet Vader, and leaves her to meet him.

In the morning, the Rebels on Endor begin battling the Empire, with the Ewoks as a powerful ally. In the skies, the remaining Rebels begin to attack the Death Star, waiting for the shields to be taken out on Endor. They soon realize that, however, it is a trap.

" 'tis a trap!"
Admiral Ackbar

Here, Luke meets the Emperor and Vader. They try and tempt him toward the dark side, and to join them. They fail, as Luke refuses, telling them that he will never give in. Watching the Rebels fight, the Emperor says that they will fail. Luke disagrees, and is insistent that they will win the war against the Empire.

On Endor, the Rebels are continuing to fight against the Empire. Finally claiming the bunker powering the Death Star's shield, they destroy the shield, bringing the Rebel pilots to attack the Death Star. Meanwhile, on the Death Star, Vader and the Emperor continue trying to tempt Luke to join the dark side of the Force. The Emperor manages to bring Luke and Darth Vader into a fight, with the two of them battling in front of him. Vader seems to be stronger than Luke, nearly bringing him to his death, but Luke soon gains an upper hand, removing one of Vader's hands. The Emperor encourages him to finish, and kill Vader, though he refuses. Angering the Emperor, he (the Emperor) attacks Luke, but Vader soon picks up Palpatine, throwing him down a shaft, killing him. Vader and Luke then leave, though Vader takes his final breath. They exit the Death Star just in time --- the Rebel pilots finally destroy the Death Star. Once Luke arrives at Endor, he burns Darth Vader's body. He then returns to the Rebels, and they and the Ewoks celebrate the Empire's destruction.

Review

thoughts about the book

I loved reading this Shakespearean adaptation of Return of the Jedi. I think that reading this put a different view on a story that is well known to myself and many others. The way that certain characters would talk was interesting, as Yoda seemed to speak in haikus, which I found unusual. I would recommend this book to anyone that's a fan of Star Wars and Shakespeare. Overall, I would give it 4.5/5 stars, as the Shakespearean dialogue did get confusing at times.
